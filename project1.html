IBM Site Redesign

IBM Systems Web site

The IBM Systems web site offers customers over 330 hardware and software products that run the world's banks, governments, and airlines. The digital property my team was assigned to fix was badly outdated and bloated – we had 7,000 URLs and a tangle of corporate jargon, hidden navigation and pages that were invisible to SEO crawlers.

The goal

Design a user-centric marketing web site for IBM Systems customers while shrinking the site footprint, migrating to a new platform, and optimizing for SEO.

The process

  • Create stakeholder map
  • Identify available research tools
  • Audit legacy web site
  • Conduct stakeholder interviews
  • Secure necessary user research tools
  • Interview users
  • Structure new site information architecture
  • Create initial wireframes
  • Test with users
  • Revise designs
  • Conduct final user tests
  • Create guidance for design team
  • Create globalization guidance
  • Document project details for future team members

The results

The new web property resulted in an engagement rate significantly higher than the company average and strong SEO results that reflected the design decisions we had made throughout the process. As a final step, I worked with our page globalization team to roll the site out to over 30 geographies and this became the standard reference for future improvements to the site.
